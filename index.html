What is a circadian wheel?

Your internal clock runs on a cycle called tau (τ). For most people it's close to 24 hours — daylight keeps it synchronized. For some people, tau runs longer: 24.5h, 25h, or more. Without anchors, sleep drifts progressively later each day, cycling through all 24 hours every few weeks. This is Non-24-Hour Sleep-Wake Disorder.

The wheel shows where you are in your personal cycle right now. Your wake time is always at the top. The outer ring maps your cycle against the 24-hour clock, colored with the actual sky at each hour — dawn, daylight, dusk, night.

The pink hand shows the current moment. The gold circle at the top is your last wake.

Built by Rikki Delaine, who has a measured tau of 25.40 hours.
